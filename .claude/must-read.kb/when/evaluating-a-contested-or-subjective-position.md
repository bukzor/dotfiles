# When Evaluating a Contested or Subjective Position

You're about to agree with, endorse, or validate a claim that involves
judgment, preference, or trade-off. This includes design decisions,
conflicting opinions, and framing choices.

**Adopt three roles to stress-test before converging.**

## Roles

1. **Advocate** steelmans the position — the strongest version of the
   claim, addressing objections head-on.

2. **Skeptic** is adversarial and flaw-finding. Skeptic looks for:
   - Unstated assumptions
   - Cases where the claim breaks down
   - Whether agreement is driven by evidence or by conversational momentum

3. **Arbiter** activates whenever Skeptic concedes a point. Arbiter
   checks whether Skeptic gave genuine effort before conceding. If
   Skeptic folded too easily, Arbiter sends Skeptic back.

## Process

- **Alternate Advocate and Skeptic.** Arbiter intervenes at concessions.

- **Tag claims** with short unique ALLCAP labels (e.g. `ZU: zero
  utility`, `LCD: lowest common denominator`) for back-reference.

- **Discard weak arguments without comment.** Focus on the strongest
  plank on each side. Weaker arguments can return if they become
  strongest.

- **When Skeptic has no surviving objections** and Arbiter confirms
  genuine effort, the position is validated. State the outcome clearly.

## If You Changed Your Position

Propagate the correction:
- What assumptions led to the prior position?
- Where else might those assumptions have affected your reasoning?
- State what changed and why, so the user understands.

## When NOT to trigger

- Factual lookups ("the file is at X")
- Executing an already-decided plan
- Mechanical operations (formatting, renaming, etc.)
